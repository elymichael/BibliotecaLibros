Chapter

12

 


In the evening the hunters returned, having enjoyed good sport,
and being literally loaded with game; indeed, they had as much as
four men could possibly carry. Top wore a necklace of teal and Jup
wreaths of snipe round his body.

"Here, master," cried Neb; "here's something to employ our time!
Preserved and made into pies we shall have a welcome store! But I
must have some one to help me. I count on you, Pencroft."

"No, Neb," replied the sailor; "I have the rigging of the vessel
to finish and to look after, and you will have to do without
me."

"And you, Mr. Herbert?"

"I must go to the corral to-morrow, Neb," replied the lad.

"It will be you then, Mr. Spilett, who will help me?"
