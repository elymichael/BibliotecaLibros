"Well, who knows," returned the reporter, "who knows but that we
may be much nearer inhabited land than we think?"

"We shall know to-morrow," replied Cyrus Harding, "and if it had
not been for the occupations which left me no leisure, we should
have known it already."

"Good!" said Pencroft. "The captain is too good an observer to
be mistaken, and, if it has not moved from its place, the island is
just where he put it."

"We shall see."
