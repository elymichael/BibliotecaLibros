"I pray heaven it may prove so, and that other eyes may find the
resemblances that are hid from mine.  Alack, I fear me the
letter spoke but too truly."

"What letter?"

"One that came from over sea, some six or seven years ago.
 It said my brother died in battle."

"It was a lie!  Call thy father—he will know me."

"One may not call the dead."

"Dead?" Miles's voice was subdued, and his lips trembled.
 "My father dead!—oh, this is heavy news.  Half my new
joy is withered now.  Prithee let me see my brother Arthur—he
will know me; he will know me and console me."

"He, also, is dead."

"God be merciful to me, a stricken man!  Gone,—both
gone—the worthy taken and the worthless spared, in me!  Ah! I
crave your mercy!—do not say the Lady Edith—"

"Is dead?  No, she lives."

"Then, God be praised, my joy is whole again!  Speed thee,
brother—let her come to me!  An' SHE say I am not myself—but
she will not; no, no, SHE will know me, I were a fool to doubt it.
Bring her—bring the old servants; they, too, will know me."
