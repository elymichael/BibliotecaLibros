
"Thou'lt not beg!" exclaimed Hugo, eyeing the King with
surprise. "Prithee, since when hast thou reformed?"

"What dost thou mean?"

"Mean? &#160;Hast thou not begged the streets of London all thy
life?"

"I? &#160;Thou idiot!"

"Spare thy compliments—thy stock will last the longer. &#160;Thy
father says thou hast begged all thy days. &#160;Mayhap he lied.
Peradventure you will even make so bold as to SAY he lied," scoffed
Hugo.

"Him YOU call my father? &#160;Yes, he lied."

"Come, play not thy merry game of madman so far, mate; use it
for thy amusement, not thy hurt. &#160;An' I tell him this, he will
scorch thee finely for it."

"Save thyself the trouble. &#160;I will tell him."

"I like thy spirit, I do in truth; but I do not admire thy
judgment. Bone-rackings and bastings be plenty enow in this life,
without going out of one's way to invite them. &#160;But a truce to
these matters; _I_ believe your father. &#160;I doubt not he can
lie; I doubt not he DOTH lie, upon occasion, for the best of us do
that; but there is no occasion here. &#160;A wise man does not
waste so good a commodity as lying for nought. &#160;But come; sith
it is thy humour to give over begging, wherewithal shall we busy
ourselves? &#160;With robbing kitchens?"
