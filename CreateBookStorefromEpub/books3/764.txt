


Book XVII


&#160;

ARGUMENT OF THE SEVENTEENTH BOOK.

Sharp contest ensues around the body of Patroclus. Hector puts
on the armor of Achilles. Menelaus, having dispatched Antilochus to
Achilles with news of the death of Patroclus, returns to the
battle, and, together with Meriones, bears Patroclus off the field,
while the Ajaces cover their retreat.
